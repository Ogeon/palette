Expand description

Oklch, a polar version of Oklab.

It is Oklab’s equivalent of CIE L*C*h°.

It’s a cylindrical color space, like HSL and HSV. This gives it the same ability to directly change the hue and colorfulness of a color, while preserving other visual aspects.

It assumes a D65 whitepoint and normal well-lit viewing conditions, like Oklab.

Fields

l: T

L is the lightness of the color. 0 gives absolute black and 1 gives the brightest white.

chroma: T

chroma is the colorfulness of the color. A color with chroma == 0 is a shade of grey. In a transformation from Oklab it is computed as chroma = √(a²+b²). chroma is unbounded

hue: OklabHue<T>

h is the hue of the color, in degrees. Decides if it’s red, blue, purple, etc.
